ENPH 253
Autonomous "Mars Rover" Robot

  • Back to home page

The goal of this 3-person project was to design, prototype, and implement a fully autonomous robot that could traverse an 8 square-foot section of floor, detect and collect empty pop cans lying around, and deposit them vertically in a designated dropoff box. I largely contribute to the software and mechanical design.

The software was implemented on an STM32 BluePill microcontroller, in the form of a state-machine in C++ to account for the various different behaviours: driving, picking up and storing a can, and depositing cans at the dropoff box. Furthermore, line-following driving was implemented with reflectance sensors located on the underside of the robot, paired with PID control.

The mechanical design went through several iterations - the main factor behind our final design was having an intermediate storage system that could store multiple cans vertically, in such a way as to make the final dropoff as simple as possible.

I also contributed to the electrical system by hand-soldering and debugging various circuits, including an H-bridge motor driver.

The biggest challenge of this project was the integration of the sub-systems. For example, integrating the software into the mechanical system introduced issues regarding inertia and imbalance that our software didn't account for before - as a result, we spent a lot of time tuning our software to compensate for these disturbances.
