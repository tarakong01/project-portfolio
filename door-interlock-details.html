Automatic Door Interlock System

One of the projects I took on during my co-op term at Kardium was designing an electromechanical system to control a 3-door entrance to the production cleanroom. The door-interlock system consisted of solid-state relays, reed sensors, electromagnetic locks, and LED lamps.

I designed the electrical schematic and wiring diagram, using digital logic to implement each of the allowed states. Components were selected with consideration to power consumption, inrush current, and operation time and frequency, then tested to ensure they met the specifications. I also had the opportunity to build the enclosure for the system using a bandsaw, drill press, and hand tools, and was heavily involved in the final installation of the system into the cleanroom.
