For my capstone project, I am designing the electrical system of a robotic AI air hockey table. The short term goal of this project is to take on human opponents and beat them in a game of air hockey; the long term purpose is to serve as a physical agent on which to train the AIâ€™s of engineering physics students in a future project course. This project is multidisciplinary as it consists of mechanical, electrical, software, and AI subteams, and each subteam has been doing thorough research to gain expertise in their field.

On the electrical side, we have been extensively researching the theory and applications of power electronics, especially motors, motor drives and controllers, switching power supplies, and batteries, and are in discussion with various companies to help with speccing our system. We have also begun preliminary circuit analysis to understand how the components are integrated together and how they behave with respect to current and voltage. Currently, major considerations include handling flyback voltage due to deceleration of the motors, supplying a steady power output in the face of large current spikes, and integrating different control methods in our electrical design, such as feedback and feedforward loops.

Before finalising any components or specifications, we are building a MATLAB Simulink model to gain an in-depth understanding of the behaviour of our system. By using mathematical principles to relate the physical load such as mass and inertia with the proposed motion trajectories, the model will be a useful and necessary guide in determining the torque and power requirements of the system, from which we will be able to move forward with component selection.
